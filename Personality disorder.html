🧠 Understanding Personality Disorders

Personality disorders involve enduring patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving that deviate from cultural expectations and cause distress or problems in functioning. They often begin in adolescence or early adulthood.

Common Types

  • Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)
  • Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD)
  • Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD)
  • Avoidant Personality Disorder (AvPD)

Signs to Look For

  • Persistent interpersonal difficulties
  • Distorted self-image
  • Difficulty regulating emotions
  • Extreme sensitivity to criticism or rejection

When to Seek Help

If your behavior or emotional struggles create significant challenges in your personal, social, or professional life, it’s important to seek support from a mental health professional.
